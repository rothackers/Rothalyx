# Code of Conduct

This project is for people who want to build and use serious tooling. The standard is straightforward: be respectful, be clear, and do not make the project harder to work on for everyone else.

## Expected Conduct

- treat contributors and users with basic respect
- keep feedback specific and technical
- assume good intent before assuming bad faith
- bring evidence when you make a strong claim
- accept review without turning every thread into an argument

## Unacceptable Conduct

- personal attacks, insults, or harassment
- discrimination of any kind
- threats, intimidation, or stalking
- publishing private information without permission
- repeatedly derailing issues, pull requests, or technical discussions
- deliberately disruptive behavior in project spaces

## Maintainer Responsibility

Maintainers may edit, lock, or remove comments, issues, or pull requests that are clearly out of bounds. They may also temporarily or permanently restrict participation when needed.

## Reporting

If you need to report a conduct problem, contact the maintainers directly through GitHub rather than starting a public argument in an issue thread.

## Practical Note

This policy is not meant to make technical discussion soft. Strong disagreement is fine. Disrespect is not.
